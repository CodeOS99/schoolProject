The Working Principle of Math

Mathematics is not merely a static set of rules; it is a dynamic system created by humans to understand and manipulate the abstract concepts of quantity, structure, space, and change.

The Order of Arithmetic

Unlike the empirical sciences where observations dictate the rules, mathematics is built upon axioms and logical deductions. If someone said 1 + 1 = 10, that 'one' would sound mentally deranged to us! However, just by changing perpectives, what if the consider the expression in base 10? Then it makes sense... That is what I mean by logical deductions. The order of arithmetic operations, such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, is a convention established for convenience rather than an objective truth. For instance, while we follow the PEMDAS/BODMAS rule (Parentheses/Brackets, Exponents/Orders, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction), it's essential to understand that this order is arbitrary and chosen to minimize the use of parentheses, making expressions more concise. There is nothing stopping you from doing addition before multiplication, however, the convention is set to this, so we follow it.
